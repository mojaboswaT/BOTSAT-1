2020: Conceptualization

2020

In 2020, Botswana embarked on the ambitious journey of developing its own satellite, BOTSAT-1. Initiated by the Botswana International University of Science and Technology (BIUST), this project aimed to enhance scientific research and open new opportunities for education and innovation across the country.

2021

2022: Integration Begins

2022

In 2022, the assembly and integration of BOTSAT-1 began, with Botswana's engineers working alongside EnduroSat to embed advanced imaging sensors for Earth observation. This phase laid the groundwork for the satellite's role in environmental monitoring and data-driven research.

2023: AIT Completed

2023

By 2023, BOTSAT-1 successfully completed Assembly, Integration, and Testing (AIT). Passing rigorous safety and performance standards, the satellite was declared flight-ready and prepared for its journey to the launch site, signaling Botswana's readiness for spaceflight.

March 2025: Launch

On March 15, 2025, BOTSAT-1 lifted off aboard a SpaceX Falcon 9 rocket from Vandenberg Space Force Base in California. The successful deployment into a Sun-synchronous orbit marked a proud and historic achievement for Botswana and its future space endeavors.

In April 2025, Botswana's ground station confirmed successful communication with BOTSAT-1. The satellite's systems were fully operational, and data transmission officially began, validating years of collaboration and engineering effort.

Mid 2025

By mid-2025, BOTSAT-1 was providing high-resolution hyperspectral images for sectors like agriculture and conservation. The satellite's insights helped farmers optimize crops, monitor ecosystems, and prepare Botswana for a data-driven sustainable future.

Late 2025: Education Integration

Late 2025

In late 2025, BOTSAT-1's mission reached classrooms across Botswana. Students and researchers gained hands-on access to real satellite data, fostering scientific curiosity and paving the way for the next generation of African space scientists and engineers.

Mission Timeline Highlights

Phase 1: Pre-launch Preparations

Before its launch, BOTSAT-1 underwent rigorous assembly, integration, and testing at EnduroSat's facilities in Sofia, Bulgaria. Botswana engineers from BIUST were actively involved, gaining valuable technical experience during this phase.

2024

Phase 2: Satellite Launch

BOTSAT-1 was successfully launched into a sun-synchronous orbit aboard SpaceX's Falcon 9 during the Transporter-13 mission. The launch marked Botswana's official entry into the space industry.

March 15, 2025

Phase 3: Initial Operations and Data Collection

After deployment, BOTSAT-1 began transmitting its first hyperspectral images back to Earth. The data supports agriculture, mining, environmental monitoring, and disaster management across Botswana.

April 2025

Phase 4: Continuous Data Transmission & Application
